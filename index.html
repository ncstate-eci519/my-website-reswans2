Favorite Ed Tech Tools

My favorite Educational/Instructional Technology are ones that allow your or your students to create content!

  • Flipgrid

    Flipgrid allows you or your students to create short videos! These are typically videos of yourself, but you can also record a whiteboard. There are plenty of fun filters, pictures, and emojis to add to the thumbnail or to your video. I also enjoy using fun graphics or the frames to add to your videos. My students enjoy making "newscasts" with their frames.

  • Scratch and Scratch, Jr.

    Scratch and Scratch, Jr. are a great way to introduce coding to an elementary, or middle school, audience using block coding. You can also make this as simple or complicated as you want with Scratch, where you can change the kind of code students are working with. Scratch, Jr. is better for lower grades and can be used to create interactive short videos and games. Scratch is better for older students, and I have used this with students to create games or interactive websites and boards (when combined with Makey Makey).

  • Sphero

    Sphero robots and sphero coding are great! My class uses these all the time to demonstrate different concepts and play games. You can also use block coding or basic html to automatically tell the sphero how and where to move. Sphero is also great for using with obstacle courses and challenges. All around fun robot to have in an elementary classroom for creative activities!

  • Chatterpix, Stikbots, and FlipaClip

    These fun tools are all for videos and stop motion video creation! Chatterpix is different than the other two, as it lets you take a picture of anything, draw a mouth and have it talk. This is great for challenging students to make an inanimate object describe itself, or have a character in a story describe their characterists. FlipaClip and Stikbots are awesome tools for elementary students to create stop motion videos. Using Stikbots, toys, and legos students can create stop motion videos in a simple format. FlipaClip allows students to create a simple stop motion animation through drawing stick figures one frame at a time.

  • Makey Makey

    You can make ANYTHING into a keyboard using makey makey. Using clips to attach objects to the computer, and turn it into a key you can do all kinds of creative projects. My students in 2nd grade last year, for example, made interactive boards describing the water cycle using makey makey. If you touched part of the board, it was attached and a students' voice would play telling you about that step in the cycle! The sky is the limit with the creativity with students creating with this tool. Combine with scratch to make your computer do different things when you use the new "keys."

  • WeVideo or Camtasia

    Not as much to say about these two, but they are my favorite video creation sites/software for teachers! It is easy to create videos where you record you, your voice, or your screen. WeVideo also has graphics and music you can add to your videos.

  • iorad

    I use this tool all the time! This is useful, especially for remote learning, to create a tutorial for any website right on the website. Share the link with your parents or students, and it will walk them through how to use that website. This year I have used it the most to show parents and students how to log in and access assignments or provide instructions.
